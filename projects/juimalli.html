Jui Malli, near Landsdowne, Uttarakhand, is a small village. Farming was becoming increasingly hard. Himjagriti â€“ an NGO, had with the village elders, marked out a piece of land to introduce a Vocational Training Institute for the women of the village. Workshop POV was asked to design six large halls that could function as multi-use spaces, which would be used to teach sewing, weaving, etc. to the women. A toilet-and-bath unit was also designed to help with the sanitary situation in the village.

Owing to the steep slope at site, the entire top side of the sitewas designed to retain with a traditional pusta (retaining wall). This traditional retaining wall helped in creating split-levels, which would step up and down to the halls on both the levels, in each block. These blocks were then connected using bridges from the pusta. The resultant residual spaces under the stilts created a community space.

The work went on hold in March of 2016, after the foundation of the first block was laid, owing to lack of funds.
